We had a bootcamp at work taking us through fundamentals of AI/ML over a few months. So, I just wanted to keep a note of learnings that I made on the way to understand the concepts. The goal was to learn enough of ML/DL so that we can apply these methods confidently in Biology. We followed the resources mentioned below and took detours learning the pre-requisites where ever needed.

I will continue reading books and adding notes to this webiste.

These are the resources that we came across were part of the bootcamp (preferred resources by the bootcamp, these were picked considering the audience was made up science folks and not techies/math people).
- Company's guide to AI/ML (internal book)
- ISLP 
- PyTorch was DL framework and for code-alongs we were following ZTM video lectures (accompanying book can be found here [link](https://www.learnpytorch.io/))
- scikit-learn for ML implementations
- [neuralnetworksanddeeplearning](http://neuralnetworksanddeeplearning.com/) cont. (this was the preferred book as it was not heavily filled with notations, topics like backpropagation were explained well but I abandonded it for other resources) 
- For understanding transformers the preferred resource was RASA's videos. Starting with [this](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yGTUuEx3GkA). These were super lucid.
- Understanding [LSTMs](https://colah.github.io/posts/2015-08-Understanding-LSTMs/)
- ClearML: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U7n9qXIBxEg
